Guidebook References (Content for Pop-ups)

Udon Shin (Shinjuku/Yoyogi, Tokyo) - Specializes in tempura and udon (a type of thicker noodle). About a 10-minute walk from Kimpton Shinjuku hotel. It's small and often has lines, reservation recommended if possible. Guidebook notes it's tiny but worth it. Address: Japan, 〒151-0053 Tokyo, Shibuya, Yoyogi, 2 Chome−20−16 相馬ビル １F.
Onigiri Manma (Shinjuku, Tokyo) - The idea here is to assemble your own Onigiri. Very simple and well-priced. About a 20-minute walk from Kimpton Shinjuku hotel. Address: Japan, 〒160-0022 Tokyo, Shinjuku City, Shinjuku, 3 Chome−14−23 マヤビル 1F.
Rokurinsha (Tokyo Station) - Famous ramen shop located on "Ramen Street" in Tokyo Station (Ichibangai B1). Usually has lines. Specialty is tsukemen, where the noodles and broth are served separately for dipping. Address: Japan, 〒100-0005 Tokyo, Chiyoda City, Marunouchi, 1 Chome−9−1 東京駅一番街 B1 東京ラーメンストリート内.
Shinjuku Gyoen Park (Shinjuku, Tokyo) - An oasis of tranquility in the frenetic Shinjuku neighborhood. An entrance fee is required. Typically closes on Mondays (unless it's a holiday, then it closes the next business day).
Tokyu Hands (Shinjuku, Tokyo) - Famous store with 7 floors offering a wide variety of goods, especially interesting for stationery and unique design products. Located in the Takashimaya Times Square South Building. Address: Japan, 〒151-8580 Tōkyō-to, Shibuya-ku, Sendagaya, 5 Chome−24−２ タカシマヤタイムズスクエア南館 2～8F.
Isetan Shinjuku (Department Store, Tokyo) - A must-visit for its "depachika" (gastronomic basement food hall). Features both local delicacies and international patisseries like Pierre Hermé. Options include buying a bento box to eat on the terrace or dining at in-store restaurants. Address: 3-chōme-14-1 Shinjuku, Shinjuku City, Tokyo 160-0022, Japan.
Unagi Hashimoto (Bunkyo City, Tokyo) - A restaurant specializing in eel (unagi), run by the same family since 1835. Located about a 15-minute taxi or 30-minute subway ride from Kimpton Shinjuku. Reservation is important. Address: 2 Chome-5-7 Suido, Bunkyo City, Tokyo 112-0005, Japan.
Shibuya Crossing (Shibuya, Tokyo) - The iconic multi-directional pedestrian crossing in front of Shibuya Station, famous for the sheer volume of people crossing simultaneously. For a good view, visit the Starbucks on the second floor overlooking the intersection.
Mugiwara Store (Shibuya, Tokyo) - The official merchandise store for the popular anime and manga series "One Piece." A must-visit for fans, offering a wide range of exclusive goods. (User-added item).
Yoyogi Park (Harajuku, Tokyo) - Home to the beautiful Meiji Shrine. On weekends, there's a chance to see traditional Shinto wedding processions. The bridge near Harajuku Station leading to the park is also a popular spot for cosplayers on Sundays.
Takeshita Street (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A bustling pedestrian street popular with young Japanese people, known for unique fashion boutiques, themed cafes, and street food like colorful cotton candy and giant crepes. Can be very crowded and touristy. Nearby Brahms Path offers a quieter alternative.
Omotesando (Harajuku, Tokyo) - Known as Tokyo's most stylish street, often compared to the Champs-Élysées. Features flagship stores of major international fashion brands like Chanel and Issey Miyake, as well as many other unique shops and cafes.
Kiddyland (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A huge toy store with multiple floors, offering a vast selection of characters, elaborate toys, and merchandise that appeals to both children and adults. Address: 6 Chome-1-９ Jingūmae, Shibuya-ku, Tōkyō-to 150-0001, Japan. (Toy Sapiens is another similar store mentioned).
Tokyu Plaza Omotesando Harajuku (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A department store known for its unique mirrored escalator entrance and a pleasant rooftop terrace (Omohara Forest) offering views of the neighborhood. A great spot for a break. Address: 4-chōme-30-3 Jingūmae, Shibuya City, Tōkyō-to 150-0001, Japan.
Ota Memorial Museum of Art (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A museum specializing in Ukiyo-e (Japanese woodblock prints). Visitors remove their shoes to walk on tatami floors. Exhibitions change regularly; check schedule as it may close on Mondays and for exhibition changes. Address: 1 Chome-10-１０ Jingumae, Shibuya, Tokyo 150-0001, Japan.
Reissue Cafe (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A fun cafe where your cappuccino foam comes decorated in 3D latte art, often featuring cute characters. Address: 3 Chome-25-7 Jingumae, Shibuya, Tokyo 150-0001, Japan.
ANAKUMA CAFE (Harajuku, Tokyo) - A unique take-out coffee stand where your coffee cup is delivered through a small opening by a bear's paw. Address: 1 Chome-23-28 Jingumae, Shibuya, Tokyo 150-0001, Japan.
Ginza Department Store Food Basements (Depachika) - The basement levels of Ginza's department stores ("depato") are dedicated to food. Expect beautifully presented (and often expensive) sweets, savory items, bento boxes, and plenty of samples. A visual and culinary experience.
Tsutaya Books (Ginza Six, Tokyo) - Considered one of the best art bookstores in the world, located within the Ginza Six shopping complex. Features an extensive collection of art books, magazines, and a sophisticated atmosphere. Address: Japan, 〒104-0061 Tokyo, Chuo City, Ginza, 6 Chome−10−1 ＳＩＸ６階.
Leica Store (Ginza, Tokyo) - A destination for photography enthusiasts, featuring Leica cameras and equipment. There is also a gallery on the second floor. Typically closes on Mondays. Address: 6 Chome-4-1 Ginza, Chūō, Tokyo 104-0061, Japan.
Ginza Lion Beer Hall (Ginza, Tokyo) - Tokyo's oldest beer hall, operating since 1934. Offers a somewhat touristy but classic experience with cold beer in a historic setting.
Ginza Sushi Ko Honten (Restaurant, Tokyo) - One of Tokyo's oldest sushi restaurants, established in 1885, and notably visited by Anthony Bourdain. Reservation is important. Address: 6 Chome-3-8 Ginza, Chuo City, Tokyo 104-0061, Japan.
Akihabara Neighborhood (Tokyo) - Known as the "Electric Town," Akihabara is the hub for electronics, anime, manga, video games, and otaku culture. Features numerous multi-story electronics stores, arcades (pachinkos), maid cafes, and specialty shops. The main street, Chuo Dori, is often closed to traffic on Sundays, becoming a pedestrian paradise.
Kanda Myojin Shrine (Akihabara area, Tokyo) - A Shinto shrine near Akihabara, known for offering blessings for modern gadgets like computers and cell phones, reflecting its proximity to the tech district. You can purchase unique tech-themed protective charms. Accessible by walking or via Ochanomizu station. Address: 2-16-2 Sotokanda, Chiyoda.
Kikanbo Ramen (Kanda/near Akihabara, Tokyo) - A modern ramen house famous for its spicy miso ramen ("Karashibi Miso Ramen"). Customers order from a vending machine and can customize the level of spiciness (kara) and numbness (shibi). Be cautious with high spice levels. Address: 2 Chome-10-9 Kajicho, Chiyoda City, Tokyo 101-0044, Japan.
Daiso Giga Funabashi (Store, Funabashi, Chiba) - A very large (6-story) branch of the popular 100-yen shop chain Daiso, offering an extensive range of products. Located less than a 5-minute walk from JR Funabashi Station. Address: Japan, 〒273-0005 Chiba, Funabashi, Honcho, 1 Chome−6−3 ギガ船橋店 ６階.
Shabusen (Yotsuya, Tokyo) - Specializes in shabu-shabu (Japanese hot pot where thin slices of meat and vegetables are cooked in broth) and sukiyaki. The Yotsuya branch is noted, though there's also one in Ginza. Address for Yotsuya: Japan, 〒160-0004 Tokyo, Shinjuku City, Yotsuya, 3 Chome−8 中央ビル ６F. Reservation for All-You-Can-Eat + Abalone Course (JPY 12,000 per person).
Roppongi Hills & Mori Tower (Roppongi, Tokyo) - A large urban development complex featuring shops, restaurants, an art museum (Mori Art Museum), an observation deck (Tokyo City View in Mori Tower), offices, and residences. Known for modern architecture and city views.
Butagumi (Nishiazabu/Roppongi, Tokyo) - A highly regarded restaurant specializing in tonkatsu (breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet). Known for offering various premium pork breeds. Address: 2 Chome-24-9 Nishiazabu, Minato City, Tokyo 106-0031, Japan. Reservation made through Toreta.
Ghibli Museum & Film Appreciation Bus Tour Details - Group tour, in English, by bus to the Ghibli Museum. Includes: tickets for the Ghibli Museum (2h30 visit), lunch (with one non-alcoholic drink) at Hotel Gajoen Tokyo, visit to the Edo-Tokyo Open Air Architectural Museum. Meet at JTB Sunrise Tours Sales Office, Keio Plaza Hotel - Main Tower - 3rd floor. Contact: +81 03 5796 5454. Payment made on your credit card, in yen, at the time of booking.
Maguro Mart (Nakano, Tokyo) - An izakaya (Japanese pub) specializing in tuna (maguro), offering various cuts and preparations, including sushi and sashimi, in an informal atmosphere. Located near Nakano Broadway, a mall known for anime and cosplay items. Address: 5 Chome-50-3 Nakano, Nakano City, Tokyo 164-0001, Japan.
Osu Shopping Street (Nagoya) - One of Nagoya's best shopping areas, a vibrant covered arcade mixing Japanese pop culture, vintage shops, electronics stores, and a variety of street food. The nearby Osu Kannon Buddhist temple is also worth a visit.
Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology (Nagoya) - Located near where the Toyota company originated, this museum details the history of the Toyota Group, from textile machinery to automobiles. An interesting visit for those interested in industrial history and cars. About a 5-minute taxi ride from Mercure Nagoya Cypress.
Nagoya Castle (Nagoya) - One of Japan's most important castles, originally built in the Edo period and reconstructed after WWII. The site includes beautiful gardens and the Hommaru Palace, which features faithfully reproduced interiors from the samurai era.
Sakae District (Nagoya) - Nagoya's premier commercial and entertainment district. Highlights include Oasis 21, a futuristic park-like structure with a distinctive glass roof ("Water Spaceship"), and the Nagoya TV Tower (now Mirai Tower).
Kyoto Yakiniku (BBQ) HIRO Kiyamachi (Restaurant, Kyoto) - A Yakiniku (Japanese BBQ) restaurant located in the Kiyamachi area. Address: Japan, 〒605-0802 Kyoto, Higashiyama Ward, Yamatocho, 7−1 祇園モーリヤビル 1F. +81 75 532 4129. Reservation made through Toreta.
Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (Kyoto) - A famous and picturesque walking path through a towering bamboo forest in the Arashiyama district. Access is often via the north gate of Tenryu-ji Temple. It's a highly anticipated sight but can be very crowded.
Tenryu-ji Temple (Arashiyama, Kyoto) - The main temple in Arashiyama and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It's renowned for its beautiful Zen garden, which utilizes the "shakkei" (borrowed scenery) technique, incorporating the surrounding mountains into its design. Address: ６８ Sagatenryūji Susukinobabachō, Ukyo Ward, Kyoto.
Shoraian (Restaurant, Arashiyama, Kyoto) - A kaiseki restaurant in Arashiyama specializing in tofu dishes, leveraging Kyoto's high-quality water. Offers beautiful views and is about a 20-minute walk from the bamboo forest. Address: 〒616-8386 京都府京都市右京区嵯峨亀ノ尾町官有地内.
Shigetsu (Restaurant, Arashiyama, Kyoto) - Located within the grounds of Tenryu-ji Temple, Shigetsu serves "shojin-ryori" (traditional Zen Buddhist vegetarian cuisine) in a very traditional atmosphere. Less than a 10-minute walk from the bamboo forest. Address: Japan, 〒616-8385 Kyoto, Ukyo Ward, Sagatenryuji Susukinobabacho, ６８.
Sen (Restaurant, Kyoto) - A one-Michelin-star Kaiseki restaurant praised for its dishes and Japanese atmosphere reflecting tea house hospitality. About a 15-minute walk from Sora Niwa Terrace Kyoto Bettei. Address: Japan, 〒600-8103 Kyoto, Shimogyo Ward, 柳馬場上ル塩竈町379. +81 75 361 8873. Reservation made through Omakase.in for the Chef's Tasting Course (JPY 33,000 per person). Credit card guarantee.
Kiyomizu-dera Temple (Higashiyama, Kyoto) - Considered Kyoto's most important temple, famous for its large wooden stage built without nails, jutting out from the hillside. It's usually bustling with visitors. The complex includes smaller shrines like Jishu Shrine (for love) and Tainai-Meguri (rebirth experience). Address: 294 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto.
Gion District (Kyoto) - Kyoto's famous geisha district, characterized by traditional wooden machiya houses, teahouses (ochaya), and exclusive restaurants. Hanamikoji Dori is a main street for exploring, and Yasaka Shrine is nearby. It's best experienced by strolling its atmospheric streets.
Wabiya Korekido (Restaurant, Gion, Kyoto) - Located on a traditional street in Gion, this restaurant serves Oyakodon (a rice bowl dish with chicken and egg). About a 10-minute walk from Sora Niwa Terrace Kyoto Bettei. Address: Japan, 〒605-0074 Kyoto, Higashiyama Ward, Gionmachi Minamigawa, 歌舞練場北側.
